BILT Model Glossary

Term Definition
BILT Model The Business and Industry Leadership Team model—“the engine” of the ITSS process—provides a structured, repeatable method for engaging employers nationwide to ensure curriculum aligns with workforce needs.
BILT‑plus An enhanced approach combining the core BILT Model’s verified KSATs with supplemental resource products (KPIs, ES, and SLOs) to deliver a more comprehensive skill‑standards package.
Delphi surveys Follow‑up rounds of questionnaires conducted among thought leaders to gain consensus on the most critical IT job clusters after initial meetings.
Employability Skills (ES) Often called “soft skills,” these are non‑technical competencies (e.g., communication, teamwork) ranked by employer SMEs to support the technical “hard” skills in the KSATs.
Job Cluster SMEs Subject matter experts (first‑line hiring managers, experienced technicians, etc.) who collaborate in cluster‑specific meetings to prioritize and verify KSAT items.
Job clusters Broad IT categories identified by thought leaders (e.g., data analytics, software development) representing the most critical areas of workforce need.
Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) Statements that specify “how good is good enough” for each highly‑prioritized KSAT, later validated in separate verification meetings.
KSATs (Knowledge, Skills, Abilities, Tasks) Core skill‑standard elements identified and ranked by employers; each KSAT describes what a well‑qualified entry‑level worker should know or be able to do 12–36 months into the future.
Pro‑forma KSAT list The draft list of KSAT items provided to employers before voting, serving as the starting point for the electronic ranking and discussion process.
Student Learning Outcomes (SLOs) Competency statements crafted by educators—aligned to the verified KSATs—to guide course and assessment design, ensuring graduates demonstrate the prioritized knowledge and skills.
Thought leaders C‑suite executives (CTOs, CIOs, futurists) convened to innovate and define the six most critical IT job clusters based on present and future workforce needs.
Verification meetings Follow‑up sessions where employer SMEs vote on and discuss KPIs (and ES) using the same electronic‑voting format applied to KSATs, to finalize performance thresholds.
